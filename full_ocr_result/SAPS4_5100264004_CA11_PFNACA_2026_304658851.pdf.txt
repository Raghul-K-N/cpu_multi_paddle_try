  1/15/2026 - Received call stating UTC oven was down.
  I then reached out to Leah who mentioned the mixing valve for the bottom burner for UTC oven #2 has split open and that they
  needed to source and install a new mixing valve.
  It is unclear if this part was able to be sourced for today.
  They then mentioned they found a different mixer on site and asked if it would work.
  I told them if the valve was approved by manufacturer then it could be installed.
  I then proceeded to site and checked in with Bobby.
  They showed me an email from the manufacturer saying the current mixing valves are Pyronics FMT mixing valves and the new
  one found is a Eclipse VSB mixer and that "it should work".
  The remaining work will be completed under OT ticket.
  1/15/2026 - Continued the following work under after hours ticket:
  Waited several hours for onsite maintenance staff to install the new valve.
  They had to make several modifications to the new valve in order for the new valve to fit, including cutting the top of the sealing
  flanges.
  Once the valve was installed we then supplied gas to the valve and several gas leaks present.
  After addressing the leaks, we then started up the bottom burner and setup the new valve at high fire to an air/gas ratio of 18.6
  02% as per manufacturer specifications.
  We then had the line operator ramp the oven down to low fire.
  I then found the air mod motor drive to the low fire position where we immediately heard a loud bang coming from the bottom
  burner, indicating a backfire situation.
  This confirms the oem mixing valve is required for safe and proper operations.
  Explained all findings to Bobby and Nils prior to departure.
